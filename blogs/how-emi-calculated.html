How EMI Is Calculated (Explained Simply With Examples)

EMI, or Equated Monthly Instalment, is the fixed amount you pay every month to repay a loan. Although EMI calculators show results instantly, understanding how the EMI is actually calculated helps you make smarter financial decisions.

::contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

1. Monthly Interest Rate — The First Step

Banks give you the annual interest rate, but EMI uses the monthly interest rate.

Example: Annual Interest Rate = 12% Monthly Rate = 12 ÷ 12 = 1% per month Decimal Form = 0.01

::contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

2. EMI Formula (Explained Simply)

All Indian banks use the same standard EMI formula: 

EMI = [ P × R × (1 + R)^N ] ÷ [ (1 + R)^N – 1 ]

Where:

  • P = Loan amount
  • R = Monthly interest rate
  • N = Number of months (tenure)
::contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

3. Worked Example — Step-by-Step EMI Calculation

Let’s calculate EMI for:

  • Loan Amount = ₹5,00,000
  • Annual Interest = 12% → Monthly Rate = 1% (0.01)
  • Tenure = 60 months

Step-by-step:

  1. (1 + R)60 = (1.01)60 ≈ 1.816
  2. Numerator = 500000 × 0.01 × 1.816 = 9080
  3. Denominator = 1.816 – 1 = 0.816
  4. EMI = 9080 ÷ 0.816 ≈ ₹11,130

So the EMI for a ₹5 lakh loan at 12% for 60 months is approximately ₹11,130 per month.

::contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

4. Why EMI Stays Constant but Interest Reduces Over Time

Although the EMI is the same every month, the interest you pay reduces gradually. This happens because interest is always calculated on the remaining principal.

::contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

5. Key Takeaways

  • EMI is fixed, but interest reduces every month
  • Monthly interest rate is simply annual rate divided by 12
  • Early years = more interest, later years = more principal
  • Part payments early in the loan save the most interest
